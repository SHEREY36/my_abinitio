# SiGeC Surface-Slab Rationale

This project starts the carbon-branching calculation after gas transport and
after MMS has produced a carbon-containing surface species. ACE+ still handles
the reactor-scale flow and surface chemistry. The atomistic model only supplies
the missing temperature-dependent constants for the competing carbon outcomes.

## Working Surface Picture

For RP-CVD SiGeC growth with SiH4/DCS, GeH4, and MMS, the wafer wall should be
treated as a Si/SiGe growth front. The exact top-surface termination depends on
precursor chemistry and temperature:

- hydride chemistry can leave H-containing surface species,
- DCS/HCl chemistry can leave Cl-containing surface species,
- at growth temperature, the reactive site is still an exposed or weakly
  passivated surface dangling-bond environment.

The first useful atomistic approximation is therefore not a pure bulk Si cell.
It is a Si(001)-like slab with a SiGe top region, optional H or Cl passivation,
and one carbon atom near the growth front.

## Why The Endpoint Structures Preserve Atom Identities

NEB needs the same atoms in the same order in the initial and final structures.
For the substitutional pathway, carbon cannot simply "turn into" a lattice atom.
Instead, the carbon atom moves into a lattice site and the displaced Si/Ge atom
moves toward the surface:

```text
C(surface) + Si/Ge(lattice) -> C(lattice) + Si/Ge(surface adatom)
```

For the interstitial pathway, the carbon atom moves from the surface into a
subsurface interstitial-like position while all lattice atoms remain present.

## Default Geometry Choices

The builder uses these conservative defaults:

- Si(001)-like slab generated by ASE from diamond Si,
- 10% Ge substitution in the top two layers,
- 25% H termination on non-carbon top sites,
- carbon initially above one open top site near the cell center,
- C-sub final state with C in the first subsurface lattice layer by default,
- C-int final state with C below the top layer in a tetrahedral-like guess.

These are not final scientific claims. They are starting structures for
relaxation. The actual MACE/DFT relaxation and NEB path should decide whether
the proposed state is stable and whether the barrier is reasonable.

## What To Vary Later

The most important sensitivity cases are:

- Ge fraction in the top growth-front layers,
- H versus Cl versus bare termination,
- carbon initial adsorption site,
- substitutional target depth (`--c-sub-layer-from-top`),
- tetrahedral versus split-interstitial final state,
- local strain or lattice constant.

Those variants can be screened with MACE and then corrected with DFT.
